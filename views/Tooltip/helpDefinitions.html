These categories are meant to be very broad; if none of them are a good fit for your dataset, please let us know with the "None of the above" option.

The data types listed here are based on the categories identified by Tamara Munzner in:

"What: Data Abstraction," in Visualization Analysis & Design. Boca Raton, FL: CRC Press, 2015.

Tables include more complex datasets such as multiple linked / relational tables or tables with multidimensional, complex, or nested cell structures.

These include trees with parent / child relationships, multivariate networks with node and/or edge attributes, as well as networks containing structures such as supernodes or hyperedges.

Fields include grids with any dimensionality, non-Euclidean coordinate systems, and fields with multidimensional attributes, such as vector or tensor fields.

Geometry includes points in space, one-dimensional lines or curves, 2D surfaces or regions, or 3D volumes.
